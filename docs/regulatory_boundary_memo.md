# Regulatory Boundary Memo

CareKin is positioned as a family care coordination tool. It helps caregivers record information, send reminders, review reports, and coordinate next steps. It must not be marketed or implemented as a diagnostic, treatment, emergency monitoring, or medical decision system without a separate regulatory review.

## Allowed positioning

- Care coordination and family communication
- Medication and routine reminders
- Daily check-in logging
- Vitals and alert logging for caregiver review
- Human-reviewed reports for family or doctor visit preparation
- Referral coordination and document sharing

## Restricted positioning

- Diagnosis or disease prediction
- Treatment recommendations
- Emergency response or real-time fall monitoring guarantees
- Automated clinical triage
- Claims of improved clinical outcomes without evidence
- AI summaries as medical conclusions

## Phase 8 gates

Before approving a clinical-adjacent feature gate in `/scale`, complete:

1. Regulatory classification review
2. Clinical safety case
3. Medical advisor review
4. Risk management process
5. Model validation if AI is involved
6. Incident reporting workflow

## Documentation rule

Each new clinical-adjacent workflow must include:

- Consent language
- Data retention notes
- Failure modes
- Escalation path
- Audit event coverage
- Human review responsibility
